A narrow bright blue beam of light descends vertically from above and strikes the ground, tracing a thin luminous line that flickers slightly as it travels. Upon impact, the contact point erupts into a dense spray of tiny brilliant blue sparks that burst outward in all directions along the ground, each spark skimming rapidly across the surface with a short bright tail. The sparks spread outward, gradually decelerating and dimming, some bouncing faintly before settling. After the initial burst, the beam rapidly thins and retracts upward, while the sparks continue to scatter and fade from bright blue to pale translucent until every spark vanishes completely.
