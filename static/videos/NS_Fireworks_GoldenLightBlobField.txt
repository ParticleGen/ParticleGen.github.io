A bright golden-white projectile launches upward from the ground with fast speed, leaving a thin sparkling trail of tiny orange embers that detach and fade behind it as it ascends. When the projectile reaches its apex and its upward momentum stops, it explodes violently into a dense spherical burst of hundreds of glittering particles all in vivid yellow and golden-white, forming a brilliant yellow firework sphere that expands outward rapidly in midair. The yellow particles slow down, hang briefly, then begin to curve gently downward under gravity, their brightness gradually dimming and their colors softening toward pale gold as they drift apart. After the yellow firework has been visible for a short while and is still fading, a second, separate explosion suddenly detonates at the same point in midair: this time, hundreds of bright crimson-red particles blast outward in a dense spherical shell, forming a vivid red firework that expands outward to a larger radius than the yellow one before it. The red particles reach their peak spread, slow down, and then also begin to fall gently under gravity, their intense red color gradually softening to burnt orange as they descend. The remaining yellow particles fade away completely first, followed shortly after by the red particles, until both bursts have fully dissipated.
