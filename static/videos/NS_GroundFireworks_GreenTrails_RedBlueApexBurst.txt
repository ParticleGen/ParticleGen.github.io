A bright golden-white projectile shoots upward from the ground with fast speed, tracing a thin sparkling trail of tiny orange embers that detach and fade behind it as it ascends. At its apex, the projectile suddenly bursts into a dense spherical cluster of hundreds of glittering particles all in vivid yellow and pale gold, forming a brilliant golden sphere that expands outward, slows down, and hangs briefly in the air. Then, from the center of this golden sphere, a second explosion erupts: countless bright crimson-red and deep orange particles blast outward at higher speed, punching through the outer golden layer and forming a larger, fiery red spherical shell that envelops the fading yellow cluster inside. The red particles radiate outward intensely, then begin to curve gently downward under gravity. Both layers then fall together: the outer red particles fade first, their color softening to burnt orange, while the inner golden core lingers a moment longer before the entire double-layered cluster dissipates completely.
